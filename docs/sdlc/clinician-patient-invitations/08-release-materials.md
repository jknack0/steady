# Clinician Patient Invitations — Release Materials

## Feature Summary

You can now invite patients directly into Steady from your dashboard. Generate a unique invite code, share it with your patient however you prefer (in person, by phone, or with an optional email), and they use it to create their account in the Steady app. You will see every invitation's status right on your patients page -- no extra screens to manage.

## What's New

- **Invite patients from your dashboard** -- Click "Invite Patient" on your patients page to generate a unique invite code in seconds.
- **Optional email notification** -- Choose to send your patient an email with the code and app download link, or share the code yourself during a session.
- **Auto-enroll in a program** -- Optionally link a program to the invitation so your patient is enrolled the moment they sign up.
- **Track invitation status at a glance** -- Pending, expired, and active patients all appear in one list with clear status badges.
- **Manage invitations from the patient view** -- Resend the email, revoke an invitation, or send a new one, all from the individual patient page.
- **Patients sign up with one code** -- Your patient enters the code in the Steady app, fills in their details, and they are in. No back-and-forth.

## How It Works

### For Clinicians

1. **Open your patients page** and click "Invite Patient" in the top right corner.
2. **Fill in the basics** -- Enter your patient's name and email address.
3. **Choose a program (optional)** -- Select a program from the dropdown if you want your patient automatically enrolled when they join. Leave it on "No program" to assign one later.
4. **Send an email (optional)** -- Check "Send email notification" if you want Steady to email the invite code to your patient. The email contains no clinical information, just the code and a link to download the app.
5. **Share the code** -- After clicking Continue, you will see a large, easy-to-read invite code (like STEADY-7X2K). Copy it with one click or read it to your patient directly.
6. **Click Done** -- The invitation appears on your patients page with a yellow "Pending" badge.
7. **Track progress** -- When your patient creates their account, the status automatically changes to active. If 30 days pass without signup, the status changes to "Expired."
8. **Take action if needed** -- From any patient's detail page, you can resend the email, revoke the invitation, or create a new one if the original expired.

### For Patients

1. **Download the Steady app** from the App Store or Google Play (or use the link in the email from your clinician).
2. **Tap "Create Account"** on the login screen.
3. **Enter your invite code** -- Type or paste the code your clinician gave you (for example, STEADY-7X2K). The app formats it automatically.
4. **Fill in your details** -- First name, last name, email, and a password.
5. **Tap "Create Account"** -- Your account is created and you are logged in immediately.
6. **Start your program** -- If your clinician linked a program to your invitation, it will be waiting for you on your home screen. Otherwise, your clinician will assign one during your next session.

## FAQ

**Q: What if my patient loses the invite code?**
A: Go to the patient's page in your dashboard and click "Resend Email" to send the code again. You can also copy the code directly from the invite widget and share it however you like.

**Q: How long is an invite code valid?**
A: Invite codes expire after 30 days. If a code expires before your patient uses it, you will see an "Expired" badge on your patients page. Just click "Send New Invite" from the patient's detail page to generate a fresh code.

**Q: Can I invite a patient without sending them an email?**
A: Yes. Leave the "Send email notification" box unchecked and the code will be displayed on screen for you to share in person, by phone, or however you prefer.

**Q: What information is included in the email to my patient?**
A: The email contains only the invite code, a link to download the app, and a short message: "Your clinician has invited you to Steady." No clinical or personal health information is included.

**Q: Can a patient sign up without an invite code?**
A: No. All patient accounts require a valid invite code. This ensures every patient in Steady is connected to a clinician from day one.

**Q: What happens if I invite someone whose email is already registered?**
A: The system will let you know that email is already in use. If the patient already has an account, they can simply log in with their existing credentials.

**Q: Can I cancel an invitation I already sent?**
A: Yes. Go to the patient's detail page and click "Revoke Invite." The code will stop working immediately. You can always send a new invitation later.

**Q: Can I assign a program after the patient has already signed up?**
A: Absolutely. If you did not select a program when creating the invitation, you can enroll the patient in a program at any time from their patient page, just like you do today.

## Talking Points

- **Streamlined onboarding**: Getting patients into Steady used to require manual coordination. Now it takes 30 seconds -- generate a code, share it, done.

- **You stay in control**: Every patient account starts with a clinician invitation. There is no self-registration, so you always know exactly who is in your caseload.

- **Zero PHI in emails**: The optional email notification is designed to meet privacy requirements. It contains a code and download link -- nothing clinical.

- **Instant program enrollment**: Link a treatment program to the invitation and your patient lands directly in their content the moment they sign up. No extra steps for you or your patient.

- **One place to manage everything**: Pending invitations, expired codes, and active patients all live on the same patients page. No separate dashboard to check.

- **Flexible sharing**: Share the code however works best for your practice -- read it aloud during a session, copy and paste it into a message, or let Steady email it for you.

- **Built for clinical workflows**: The invite system fits naturally into how clinicians already onboard patients. It does not require new processes or training -- just one button and one code.
